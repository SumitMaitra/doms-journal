Submission Guidelines

Important: Detailed submission guidelines will be published soon. For urgent queries, please contact us.

Types of Submissions

NITI welcomes the following types of contributions:

Original Research Articles

Empirical and theoretical research advancing scholarly knowledge.

Conceptual Papers

Theoretical frameworks and conceptual models.

Case Studies

In-depth analysis of real-world situations and contexts.

Policy Analyses

Critical examination of policy design and implementation.

Manuscript Preparation

General Guidelines
  • Language: Manuscripts must be written in English
  • Length: Typically 6,000-8,000 words (excluding references)
  • Format: Microsoft Word or LaTeX format
  • Style: APA 7th edition or as specified
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, double-spaced

Submission Process

1

Peer Review Process

All submissions undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process. Manuscripts are evaluated based on:

  • Relevance to the journal's aims and scope
  • Originality and significance of contribution
  • Methodological rigor and validity
  • Clarity of presentation and writing quality
  • Practical and theoretical implications

Publication Ethics

Ethical Standards

Authors must ensure that:

  • The work is original and has not been published elsewhere
  • All authors have made substantial contributions to the work
  • All sources are properly cited and referenced
  • Any conflicts of interest are disclosed
  • Research involving human subjects has received ethical approval
  • Data and materials are available for verification if requested

Copyright and Licensing

Authors retain copyright of their work while granting the journal the right to first publication. Details on licensing terms will be provided upon acceptance.
